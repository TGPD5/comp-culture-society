▶ NOTE — STATISTICAL METHODS DIV., CAPT. E.L. MORSE

Each candidate's physical dimensions are drawn from the general male population, which follows a normal distribution for each measurement — each entirely independent of the others. On the bar charts above, the center of the amber zone corresponds to the population mean for that dimension — the value most men cluster around. The amber zone covers 0.385 standard deviations to each side of the mean — capturing roughly 30% of the male population per measurement: a perfectly reasonable window into which we expect a good number of men to fall.
